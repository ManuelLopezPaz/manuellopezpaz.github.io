This is my story — alongside a few photos from Buenos Aires.

I've always been a curious person. When something catches my attention, I tend to look it up and understand the story behind it. That habit of asking "why" is what led me toward data and analysis.

In high school, I became interested in business through projects focused on decisions and outcomes. Working with data felt more natural than anything else.

While studying business, I realized I was most engaged in quantitative subjects, especially statistics. It pushed me to start exploring data on my own.

Because I've always followed sports closely, it felt natural to start there. I began working with datasets, learning Python, and exploring patterns behind performance and results.

Through this process, I realized I was more interested in using data to understand decisions than just describing outcomes. That shift is what drew me to econometrics and operations research.

I aim to work in environments where data and strategy meet — whether in sports, technology, or other complex industries.

Outside of academics, I enjoy football, going to the gym, playing Catan with friends, and exploring new technologies.
